Classical music spans an enormous range of tempo and energy, from slow meditative pieces under 60 BPM to intense orchestral works above 160 BPM, but the catalog in this system leans toward quieter, piano-forward pieces with low to moderate energy. Instrumentation is entirely acoustic, featuring piano, strings, and orchestral arrangements that create a sense of depth and emotional complexity without electronic elements. Common moods include sad, focused, relaxed, and romantic, and classical music is particularly effective for studying, meditation, reading, and any context that benefits from emotional depth without distraction. It appeals to listeners who want something emotionally rich but without lyrics competing for attention. Classical overlaps with ambient music in its atmospheric qualities, with jazz in its acoustic instrumentation, and with lofi in its use as background listening for concentration.
