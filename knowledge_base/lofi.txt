Lofi hip hop sits in the range of 60 to 90 BPM with deliberately low energy, featuring muted drums, warm piano or guitar loops, vinyl crackle, and ambient background textures that create a sense of intimacy. The genre was designed for extended listening during tasks that require concentration, which is why it avoids jarring transitions and keeps energy levels flat and steady. Common moods are chill, focused, and gently melancholic, and it is most often used for studying, coding, late-night work, or background listening while reading. Lofi appeals to listeners who find silence too empty but louder music too distracting. It overlaps with jazz in its use of chord voicings and improvised-feeling phrasing, with ambient music in its low-intensity texture, and with hip hop in its drum pattern origins.
