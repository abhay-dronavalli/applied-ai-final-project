Jazz spans a wide tempo range but most conversational and coffeehouse jazz sits between 80 and 140 BPM with moderate energy, featuring piano, upright bass, brushed drums, and horns in arrangements that feel spontaneous and warm. Improvisation is central to jazz, giving it a sense of human unpredictability that stands apart from more produced genres. Common moods include relaxed, romantic, and sophisticated, and jazz works well in cafes, dinner settings, late evenings, and any context where the listener wants something emotionally warm but not distracting. It appeals to listeners who appreciate musicianship and complexity without requiring high energy. Jazz overlaps with lofi in its chord vocabulary and laid-back feel, with r&b in its rhythm section influence, and with classical in its acoustic instrumentation and harmonic depth.
