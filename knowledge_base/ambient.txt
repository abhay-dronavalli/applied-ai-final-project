Ambient music is defined by texture over melody, using synthesizers, field recordings, and slowly evolving layers of sound that may have no clear beat or tempo at all. Energy is very low by design, creating an enveloping atmosphere rather than directing the listener's attention to any single element. Common moods include chill, dreamy, relaxed, and focused, and the genre excels in contexts like meditation, sleep preparation, deep work, and background listening where distraction would be counterproductive. Ambient suits listeners who want music that recedes into the environment rather than competing with their thoughts. It overlaps with classical music in its orchestral textures and dynamic range, with lofi in its low-intensity role, and with synthwave in its use of synthesizer-heavy production.
