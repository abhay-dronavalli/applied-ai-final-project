R&B typically runs between 60 and 100 BPM with moderate energy, built on smooth, expressive vocals layered over bass-heavy production that blends synthesizers, programmed drums, and live instrumentation in a polished and emotionally direct style. The genre prioritizes feel and vocal performance over complexity, creating music that is simultaneously intimate and sophisticated. Common moods include romantic, moody, sensual, and melancholic, making r&b a natural fit for late evenings, romantic settings, and any situation that calls for something warm and emotionally present. It appeals to listeners who want music that centers the human voice and emotional honesty. R&B overlaps with hip hop in its production origins and bass-forward sound, with jazz in its harmonic sophistication and vocal influence, and with pop in its melodic hooks and mainstream appeal.
