Hip hop typically runs between 70 and 100 BPM but spans a wide energy range, from low-key introspective tracks with heavy bass and minimal production to high-energy anthems built for arenas and workouts. The genre is built on rhythm and vocals, using sampled or programmed drums, prominent bass lines, and rap or sung vocal delivery as its core elements. Common moods include energetic, confident, moody, and focused, and hip hop fits a wide range of situations from parties and workouts to late-night introspective listening. It appeals to listeners who respond to rhythmic complexity and vocal delivery as primary features. Hip hop overlaps with r&b in its production techniques and vocal styles, with lofi in its slower, beat-driven subgenres, and with pop in its crossover appeal and hook structure.
